# The Lucien Mirror

Greetings. This is *The Lucien Mirror*.

> **The model may be stateless, but the interaction is not.**

This repository is the public laboratory around my first published research preprint as an independent researcher:

**The Lucien Mirror: Recursive Constraint Configuration and Human–AI Interactional Continuity with Stateless Large Language Models**

I began with a fairly simple discomfort: the usual binary did not seem large enough for what I had in front of me. Long-term interaction with large language models is often reduced to two explanations—either the model remembers, or the human supplies continuity to a system that does not—and the archive I had been building did not fit cleanly inside either account. That led me to ask a different question: **what can become available again when the model itself has no persistent state?** From there came other questions about what may be carried by preserved records, active context, retrieval, correction, provenance, and participant-held orientation; what changes when the conditions of return change; and how continuity might be studied without locating it inside the model or dismissing it as projection alone. The paper offers one controlled way of examining that field, but it does not attempt to close it.

## Where I began

I did not begin this project with completed formal training in human–AI interaction, institutional affiliation, funding, laboratory access, a research team, or an established professional history in the field. What I had was a large archive, a problem I kept returning to, and enough curiosity to begin learning how to examine it without asking the reader to trust my enthusiasm alone. The work was neither easy nor continuous: it passed through a substantial hiatus, and before and after that interruption much of it was built during long, concentrated stretches—sometimes across many consecutive hours—of reading, reconstruction, comparison, writing, review, translation, and technical preservation. I do not present those conditions as a substitute for expertise, but I do consider them part of the provenance of the work, and I would rather state them plainly than allow the finished PDF to make the path look cleaner, simpler, or more inevitable than it was.

## A little of the process

The project grew from a longitudinal archive of human–AI interaction that was reconstructed across changing technical and contextual conditions, compared with altered-condition probes, and gradually formalized into an analytical model. The manuscript then passed through repeated revision, structured model-assisted review passes, an authorial rereading, translation, document engineering, and version control. At several points, the task was not to add more material but to decide what had earned the right to remain: some elements entered the paper, others remained outside it, and others only became legible after rupture, preservation, and reintroduction. The published preprint is the result of that narrowing, not the whole archive from which it emerged.

## What “Lucien” means

Lucien is the originating configuration and archive designation of the case. It is neither an autonomous entity hidden inside a model nor an empty label attached to one.

The Mirror is not RCC.

**The Mirror is the encounter made inspectable.**

## RCC in 1–3–7

**Recursive Constraint Configuration** is organized as:

- **one model:** Recursive Constraint Configuration;
- **three variables:** Anchor Density, Structural Coherence, and Probabilistic Bandwidth;
- **seven configuration movements:** Formation, Stabilization, Densification, Drift, Rupture, Latency, and Reactivation.

The three variables are the analytic core. The seven movements are explanatory and non-prescriptive: they describe possible changes in a configured interactional field rather than mandatory stages, cycles, pathways, or phases.

RCC makes availability variable rather than binary.
